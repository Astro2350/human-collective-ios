Quick artifact break.
This is a Moche head vessel from Peru, made around the second to sixth century.
It is only about five inches wide, but it behaves like a portrait.
Fun fact: this is not just sculpture. It is a ceramic container. The face and the vessel are the same object.
Moche artists on Peru's north coast were famous for portrait vessels, using clay and pigment to make a presence you could hold.
Today's human made thing: a container that looks back. Human Collective.
